Semantic Markup In HTML

Semantic literally means "Meaning". There are two types of tags in HTML:
  1. Semantic Tags: These are the tags that provide formatting such that just by looking at the tag, we can guess the semantic meaning of the content inside it. For example, consider the heading tags. Just by looking at them, we can easily figure out the contextual meaning of the content inside it.
  2. Non-Semantic Tags: These are the tags whose formatting is not that clear with respect to the contextual meaning. These tags are rather used for the grouping and targetting of style for the content of the webpage. Consider the div tag. By looking at the tag you cannot say what it is doing.

Why Use Semantic Markup

  • Meaningful And Structured Layout

  • Search Engine Optimization

  • Screen Reader Support
